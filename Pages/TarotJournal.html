Tarot Journal

#HTML #CSS #JavaScript #Open Source #Bullet Journal

Timeline: June 2023 - Present

Overview

Tarot Journal is a web app that serves as a curated Tarot card reference and streamlined recording tool for tarot readings. Users can design their spread and draw from a virtual deck or record draws from a physical deck, then access the information for each card and record their personal notes. They can then export a convenient print-format page of their results.

This ongoing, open source* project will continue to evolve as I add new features.

Around October 2022, I discovered an interest in using Tarot reading as a tool for journaling. In tarot readings, you draw cards to place in specific layouts, called spreads, with meanings for the positions. Each card in the 78-card deck also has its own meaning, which can be a overwhelming for a beginner.

I wanted a tool that allowed me to neatly record my thoughts, and gave me intuitive access to card descriptions. Since I couldn't find one that met my needs, I decided to develop my own and make it available to others.

*If you have any questions, suggestions for improvements, or want to contribute to the project, please contact me!

Roles

This project serves as a testing ground for refreshing my HTML, CSS, JS and JSON knowledge. All code, including the parser to convert from CSV data, was my own creation. Eventually, I plan to write my own descriptions and interpretations of the cards. Currently, the information comes from A Little Spark of Joy's blog. The artwork is my edit of the original Smith-Waite Tarot deck.
