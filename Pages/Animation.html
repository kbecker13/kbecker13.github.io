Animation and 3D Works

#Blender #AdobeAnimate #Photoshop #Illustrator #Maya #Procreate

Timeline: 2006-Present

Overview

I first discovered my love of digital animation in my high school Digital Media class. Shortly after, that love was cemented with a gifted copy of Macromedia Flash 8 (the great-grandaddy of Adobe Animate), which I used to create short music videos, lip sync animations, and Flash games. I have continued to pursue this passion in the intervening years in a variety of mediums and softwares.

Something Blue is an animated short based on the classic wedding poem. Around the time of its creation, I had recently lost a loved one, and was surrounded by friends getting engaged and married. I was inspired to tell a story that combined that heartbreak of loss with the joy of a long life lived together.

I often use 3D modeling as part of my planning process for sculptures, illustrations, home upgrades, and furniture plans. It allows for perfect measurements and visualizing how complex ideas can come together.

I also enjoy modeling as its own art form. CGI is constantly evolving at a breakneck speed, so there's always some new technique to learn and try.

Roles

I have developed animations in every stage of the pipeline: the storyboard, look development, modeling, texturing, rigging, animating, lighting, rendering and post-processing. I work in both 2D and 3D, and specialize in multimedia creation.
